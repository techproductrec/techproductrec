VU GLOLED TV


The Vu GLOLED TV is characterized by its distinctive features and design elements that set it apart from other models. Sporting a sleek bezel-less design with slim bezels on three sides, it exudes a premium look. The fourth side features slim bezels as well, maintaining a consistent aesthetic. Notably, the TV incorporates Farfield microphones discreetly positioned below the branding, while all connectivity options are conveniently located at the back, including three HDMI ports with advanced features like eARC, ALLM, and VRR 48Hz to 60Hz. Additionally, it supports Bluetooth 5.1 and dual-band Wi-Fi, offering seamless connectivity options.

The panel of the Vu GLOLED TV boasts an 8-bit FRC VA DLED display with a peak HDR brightness of 400 nits, meeting the brand's claims. Supporting various HDR formats such as HDR10, HDR10+, HLG, and Dolby Vision IQ, the TV offers impressive picture quality. Dolby Vision IQ enhances the viewing experience by adjusting picture quality and brightness according to the ambient lighting conditions, ensuring optimal visual performance. The blacks produced by the VA panel are commendable, although not on par with OLED panels. The AI picture quality engine achieves up to 94% NTSC color gamut, resulting in vivid and punchy colors. However, some users may find the colors slightly oversaturated, particularly in red and green tones.

Regarding HDR performance, the TV supports HDR10+ and Dolby Vision IQ, although there may be issues with HDR10+ content, such as bending and patching. Nonetheless, Dolby Vision content delivers brilliant performance, and 4K content is rendered superbly without any issues. Running on Android 11 with built-in Chromecast support, the TV offers access to a wide range of pre-downloaded default Android apps, with additional apps available for download from the Play Store. The TV provides 16 GB of gross storage, with 7.3 GB of net usable storage.

In terms of upscaling, the TV delivers average performance for SD content, while upscaling HD channels is impressive. USB upscaling results in excellent clarity for 1080p and 4K content, with native 4K content appearing particularly stunning. The audio quality is described as amazing, with a built-in subwoofer on the back providing enhanced bass. However, some users may find the audio profile slightly bass-heavy, preferring a more balanced audio experience.

Despite its strengths, the Vu GLOLED TV has limitations such as narrow viewing angles typical of VA panels and potential issues like backlight glow and color consistency bugs. Nevertheless, it offers an overall impressive picture quality and features, particularly considering its competitive price range. It is recommended for users who prioritize front-viewing experiences and appreciate the TV's gaming capabilities, including ALLM support and a VRR of 48Hz to 60Hz. Additionally, the TV features software-based MEMC, providing a good option for budget VA panel TVs.

In conclusion, the Vu GLOLED TV stands out as a compelling option for users seeking a budget-friendly yet feature-rich television. While it may not compete with higher-end models from other brands, it offers a satisfactory viewing experience with impressive picture quality and connectivity options. It is essential for prospective buyers to consider the TV's limitations and manage expectations accordingly, focusing on its strengths within its price range.
