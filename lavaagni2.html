Lava Agni 2 5G


The Lava Agni 2 marks a significant evolution in the Agni-series lineup, coming almost two years after its predecessor, the first-gen Lava Agni. With notable changes in design and specifications, this device offers a compelling package at its price point. Here's a detailed review based on my experience with the device.

Verdict While the Lava Agni 2 has some limitations in terms of battery life and camera performance, it excels in delivering a solid everyday user experience with its immersive display, fast charging capabilities, and user-friendly software. Its standout design further adds to its appeal.

Design and Display The Lava Agni 2 boasts a striking design characterized by a curved display and a prominent circular rear camera module. The frosted glass back not only adds to the aesthetic appeal but also helps in maintaining a clean appearance by repelling fingerprints and smudges. However, the device's weight of 210 grams may be a downside for some users, impacting prolonged multimedia usage. On the display front, the device features a 6.78-inch AMOLED panel with FHD+ resolution and up to 120Hz refresh rate. The display offers vibrant colors, thin bezels, and decent sunlight legibility, contributing to an enjoyable viewing experience.

Camera Equipped with a quad-rear camera setup, including a 50MP primary sensor, an 8MP ultra-wide lens, and two 2MP sensors for depth and macro shots, the Lava Agni 2 delivers satisfactory camera performance. While the primary sensor captures detailed shots in daylight, low-light conditions may result in images with noise and oversaturated colors. The ultra-wide camera performs adequately in well-lit environments, albeit with a drop in details.

Performance and Software Powered by the MediaTek Dimensity 7050 SoC, the Lava Agni 2 offers smooth performance for everyday tasks and multitasking. It handles gaming reasonably well, although prolonged gameplay sessions may lead to slight performance dips due to heat generation. The device runs on Android 13 with a light custom skin, providing a user-friendly experience with minimal bloatware and intrusive notifications.

Battery and Fast Charging Speeds With a 4,700mAh battery, the Lava Agni 2 offers decent battery life, providing approximately 5 hours of screen-on-time for regular users. However, heavy users may find the battery life underwhelming. Fortunately, the device supports 66W fast charging, enabling quick charging times of around 45-50 minutes from empty to full.

Final Verdict The Lava Agni 2 earns praise for its striking design, vibrant AMOLED display, user-friendly software, and solid everyday performance. However, its shortcomings in battery life and camera performance may deter some users. Overall, it's a worthy consideration for those seeking an affordable yet feature-packed smartphone experience.

Pros: Striking design with curved display Vibrant AMOLED display User-friendly software with minimal bloatware Good performance for everyday use

Cons: Underwhelming battery life Non-expandable storage Cameras struggle in low-light conditions
