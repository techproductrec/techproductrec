Lava Storm 5G


In the bustling budget smartphone market, finding a device that balances essential features with an overall user-friendly experience can be a daunting task. Recently, I had the opportunity to test the Lava Storm 5G, and it proved to be a promising contender in the budget smartphone arena. Here's my comprehensive review.

Design and Build The Lava Storm 5G boasts a sleek and understated design reminiscent of recent Samsung smartphones. Its distinct camera array, featuring a dual-camera setup and an LED flash cleverly integrated to resemble a camera, adds a unique touch. Unlike its predecessors, such as the Blaze 2, which focused on flashy aesthetics, the Storm prioritizes functionality over flamboyance. Encased in a glass sandwich design, it feels robust in hand, albeit slightly heavier at 214 grams. The inclusion of a microSD card slot, a 3.5mm headphone jack, and dual nano SIM card slots with 5G support further enhance its appeal.

Display Equipped with a 6.78-inch FHD+ IPS display boasting a 120Hz refresh rate, the Lava Storm 5G offers a delightful viewing experience. The display's vivid mode, though slightly saturated by default, contributes to an immersive visual experience. However, uneven bezels, particularly the thicker bottom bezel, lend a dated look to the device. Nonetheless, for multimedia enthusiasts and gamers, the Storm's vibrant display sets it apart from competitors like the Redmi 12 5G with its 90Hz display.

Camera Performance In contrast to many budget smartphones cluttered with unnecessary cameras, the Lava Storm 5G opts for a streamlined approach. It features a 50MP primary sensor and an 8MP ultra-wide-angle lens, alongside a 16MP selfie camera. Notably, the inclusion of a Google Lens shortcut outside the camera app enhances user convenience. The camera performance is commendable, especially in daylight conditions, capturing bright and vivid images. However, details may appear soft when zoomed in, mitigated by the UHD mode's improved clarity. Portrait mode, while effective, exhibits minor edge detection issues.

Performance Powered by the MediaTek Dimensity 6080 SoC coupled with 8GB of RAM and 128GB of internal storage, the Lava Storm 5G delivers a smooth and responsive performance. With an AnTuTu benchmark score exceeding 4 lakh points, day-to-day usage feels snappy. Gaming enthusiasts can enjoy titles like BGMI at HD settings without encountering lag issues. Remarkably, even during extended gaming sessions, the device maintains optimal temperature levels without overheating, a testament to its efficient thermal management.

Software and Connectivity The Lava Storm 5G offers a clutter-free user experience with stock Android, devoid of bloatware or third-party apps. However, it ships with Android 13, which may disappoint users expecting the latest iteration. On the connectivity front, the device seamlessly integrates with the Jio 5G network, ensuring reliable voice and video calls.

Battery Life The device houses a robust 5,000mAh battery with support for 33W fast charging, promising prolonged usage durations. In practical usage scenarios involving light gaming, video streaming, and social media browsing, the device delivers an average screen-on-time of approximately 7 hours. The inclusion of a fast charging adapter further enhances convenience, ensuring minimal downtime during charging sessions.

Verdict The Lava Storm 5G emerges as a compelling entry-level smartphone, offering a harmonious blend of essential features and an elegant design. While it lacks a stereo speaker setup, its simplistic design and stock Android UI make it an attractive choice for users seeking an affordable 5G smartphone.
