Redmi 12 5G


Xiaomi's Redmi 12 5G aims to cater to budget-conscious consumers seeking affordable 5G connectivity. Priced at Rs. 11,999 for the base variant, this smartphone offers a compelling package for those prioritizing value. Let's delve into the details to see if Xiaomi delivers on its promises.

Price and Variants: The Redmi 12 5G is available in multiple variants, starting from 4GB of RAM with 128GB of storage priced at Rs. 11,999. The top variant, which I've been testing, comes with 8GB of RAM and 256GB of storage, priced at Rs. 15,499. It's worth noting that Xiaomi offers a 256GB storage option, providing ample storage for users. However, this pricing places it in close proximity to the Redmi Note 12 5G, which boasts superior features like a Super AMOLED display and faster charging.

Design: Sporting a polycarbonate chassis with a glass back panel, the Redmi 12 5G offers a sleek design at an affordable price point. The chrome rings around the camera lenses add a touch of elegance. Despite its large 6.79-inch display, the smartphone feels well-balanced and relatively thin at 8.17mm. However, the flat sides may cause discomfort during prolonged use, necessitating the use of a case. The back panel is prone to fingerprints and smudges, requiring frequent cleaning.

Display: The Redmi 12 5G features a 6.79-inch Full-HD+ LCD display with a 90Hz refresh rate. While the display is adequate for basic tasks, the absence of an AMOLED screen is noticeable when watching content. The display offers decent brightness levels indoors but struggles outdoors, affecting legibility.

Cameras: Equipped with a dual-rear camera setup, including a 50-megapixel primary camera, the Redmi 12 5G delivers satisfactory performance in well-lit conditions. However, low-light photography reveals limitations, with a noticeable drop in detail and performance. The absence of an ultra-wide sensor is a drawback. The 8-megapixel front camera captures detailed images in daylight but struggles in low-light conditions.

Battery and Charging: One of the standout features of the Redmi 12 5G is its 5,000mAh battery, providing ample power for day-to-day tasks and content consumption. However, the 18W charging feels slow compared to competitors, requiring extended charging times. In our battery loop test, the smartphone demonstrated impressive endurance, lasting 17 hours and 49 minutes.

Performance: Powered by Qualcomm's Snapdragon 4 Gen 2 SoC, the Redmi 12 5G delivers smooth performance for most apps but struggles with competitive gaming. While day-to-day usage is lag-free, gaming enthusiasts may find the performance underwhelming. MIUI 14, based on Android 13, offers a smooth user experience despite the presence of preinstalled apps.

Verdict: The Redmi 12 5G presents a compelling option for budget-conscious consumers seeking affordable 5G connectivity. With its large battery, decent display, and conventional design, it checks most of the basic boxes for a budget smartphone. However, those prioritizing display quality and camera performance may find better alternatives like the Redmi Note 12 5G for a slightly higher price. Overall, the Redmi 12 5G offers good value for its price range and serves as a viable entry-level 5G smartphone option.
