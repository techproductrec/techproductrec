LG UR7500


LG has unveiled its UR series for 2023.

Regarding the appearance, LG has completely revamped the bezel design of the UR series, significantly reducing bezel size compared to the UQ series. The TVs come with plastic bodies and stands, with connectivity options conveniently located at the back, including three HDMI ports (one of which is an eARC 2.1 port), two USB ports, an optical port, Bluetooth 5.0, and dual-band WiFi support. While the standard remote lacks voice assistant functionality, users have the option to purchase a Magic Remote separately.

One notable aspect of the UR 7500 series is the uncertainty regarding the panel type. LG ships both IPS and VA panels, with no guarantee of which panel type a buyer will receive, except for the 50-inch size, which is guaranteed to have a VA panel.

In terms of HDR support, the TVs offer HDR10 and HLG support, as well as LG's proprietary HDR10 Pro. While Dolby Vision support is absent, the TVs handle Dolby Vision content well, thanks to their improved HDR capabilities and clarity.

The upscaling performance for content from set-top boxes and USB sources is praised for its noticeable improvement, particularly in upscaling SD content. The TVs' gaming features, including Auto Low Latency Mode and HGiG support, contribute to a satisfying gaming experience with minimal input lag.

Audio quality is described as satisfactory for basic viewing, although not cinematic.

Despite some drawbacks, such as the lack of Dolby Vision support and unchanged picture processor, the reviewer highlights several improvements in the UR series, including enhanced HDR capabilities, better upscaling, reduced motion blur, and improved viewing angles compared to the UQ series. We ultimately recommend the UR series over the UQ series for those seeking improved picture quality and overall value.

Overall, the LG UR 7500 series offers a compelling mix of features, performance, and affordability, making it a viable option for consumers in the market for a new television.
